Hon'ble Alok Mathur,J.
1. Heard Ms. Rushida Farheen, learned counsel for the applicant as well as Sri Akhil Bajpai, learned
counsel on behalf of opposite party no.2.
2. The present application U/S 482 Cr.P.C. (Now U/S 528 of BNSS Act, 2023) has been filed for
quashing the impugned summoning order dated 06.07.2023 passed by learned Additional Court
No.5, Lucknow in Complaint Case No.38646/2023, in re: Sanjeev Kumar Gupta vs. Manish Gupta,
under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, Police Station - Aliganj, District - Lucknow.

3. It has been jointly submitted by learned counsel for both the parties that during the mediation
proceedings, a settlement has been arrived at between the parties which has been enclosed along
with the report of the mediator. The settlement agreement reads as under:-
"This SETTLEMENT AGREEMENT entered into on 04.04.2025, between Manish
Kumar Gupta S/O Shri Babu Ram Gupta R/O Munnu Ganj Gola Gokaran Nath,
District Khiri, Uttar Pradesh (First Party) and Sanjeev Kumar Gupta S/O Late Prithvi
Chandra Gupta R/O B-12, Sec-B, Aliganj, Lucknow U.P. (Second Party).
WHEREAS
1. Disputes and differences had arisen between the Parties hereto and APPLICATION
U/s 482 No.108 of 2025 (Manish Kumar Gupta & Others Vs State of U.P. & Another)
was filed before Allahabad High Court, sitting at Lucknow.
2. The matter was referred to Mediation/Conciliation vide an order dated 08.01.2025
passed by Hon'ble Mr. Justice Kaurnesh Singh Pawar.
3. The parties agreed that Mr. Mayankar Singh, Advocate and Mr. Mudit Agarwal,
Advocate would act as their Mediators/Conciliators.
4. Several meetings were held during the process of Conciliation/Mediation and the
parties, with the assistance of the Mediators/ Conciliators, have voluntarily arrived at
an amicable solution resolving all their dispute and differences.
5. The parties hereto confirm and declare that they have voluntarily and of their own
free will arrived at this Settlement Agreement in the presence of the
Mediators/Conciliators and there is no coercion or undue influence on either of the
parties of any kind whatsoever in arriving at this settlement agreement.
6. The following settlement has been arrived at between the Parties hereto:-
A. That the First Party has agreed to pay and the Second Party has agreed to receive a
sum of Rs.2,50,000/- (Rupees Two Lakhs Fifty Thousand only) towards one time full
and final settlement of all the claims of the Second Party against the First Party with
regard to Complaint Case No.38646 of 2023 (Sanjeev Kumar Gupta Vs. Manish
Kumar Gupta) U/S 138 of N. I. Act, P.S.-Aliganj, District-Lucknow pending before
Additional Court No.5, Lucknow.
B. That the First Party has handed over a Demand Draft No.683220 dated
03.04.2025 amounting to Rs.2,50,000/- (Rupees Two Lakhs Fifty Thousand only)
drawn on Punjab National Bank Branch Gola Gokaran Nath Lakhimpur Kheri to the
Second Party toward aforementioned payment of Rs.2,50,000/- (Rupees Two Lakhs
Fifty Thousand only).

C. That the Second Party has agreed not to pursue the case bearing Complaint Case
No.38646 of 2023 (Sanjeev Kumar Gupta Vs. Manish Kumar Gupta) U/S 138 of N. I.
Act, P. S.-Aliganj, District-Lucknow pending before Additional Court No.5, Lucknow.
The Second Party has also agreed that he would not be having any objection if the
APPLICATION U/s 482 No.108 of 2025 (Manish Kumar Gupta & Others Vs State of
U.P. & Another) is decided by the Hon'ble Court in terms of this settlement
agreement.
D. That it is also agreed between the parties that they shall not institute any case in
future in the form of criminal or civil proceedings against each other in respect of the
present dispute under Complaint Case No.38646 of 2023 (Sanjeev Kumar Gupta Vs.
Manish Kumar Gupta) U/S 138 of N. I. Act, P. S.-Aliganj, District-Lucknow pending
before Additional Court No.5, Lucknow and if any proceeding has already been
initiated, both the parties would get it disposed off in terms of this settlement
Agreement.
E. That both the parties shall be bound by the terms and conditions of this Settlement
in strict sense. In case of any default, the party committing the default shall be liable
for playing fraud with the Court, hence for contempt of the Court.
7. By signing this Agreement the Parties hereto state that they have no further claims
or demands against each other with respect to the matter involved in APPLICATION
U/s 482 No.108 of 2025 (Manish Kumar Gupta & Others Vs State of U.P. & Another)
and all disputes and differences in this regard have been amicably settled by the
Parties hereto through the process of Conciliation Mediation."
4. The petition is allowed in terms of the settlement agreement arrived at between the parties and
the impugned summoning order dated 06.07.2023 passed by learned Additional Court No.5,
Lucknow in Complaint Case No.38646/2023, in re: Sanjeev Kumar Gupta vs. Manish Gupta, under
Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, Police Station - Aliganj, District - Lucknow are
quashed. The settlement agreement dated 04.04.2025 shall remain part of this order.
