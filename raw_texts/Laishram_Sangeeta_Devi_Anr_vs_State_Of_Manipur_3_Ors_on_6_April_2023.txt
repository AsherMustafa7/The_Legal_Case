BEFORE HON'BLE THE ACTING CHIEF JUSTICE MR. MV MURALIDARAN 06.04.2023 [1]
Heard Mr. N. Jotendro, learned senior counsel for the petitioners; Mr. U. Augusta, learned junior
counsel to Mr. Y. Nirmolchand, learned senior counsel for the respondent No.3 and Mr. M. Rarry,
learned Special Counsel appearing for the State has taken notice and appeared before this Court. [2]
Both the counsel for the parties agreed for disposal of the writ petition at the motion stage itself. [3]
Mr. Jotendro, learned senior counsel for the petitioners represented that the present writ petition
has been filed seeking the prayers as follows :
"(i) issue rule and call for records;
(ii) issue writ in the nature of Certiorari or

Commissioner (MAHUD), Government of Manipur (Annexure-A/15 to the writ petition) and Letter
dated 29.3.2023 (Annexure-A/18 to the writ petition) issued by the Joint Secretary (MAHUD),

[4] He further argued that though the petitioners have filed a representation dated 19.10.2022
requesting the authority to review/recall the decisions of the State Government to reserved IMC
Ward No. 19 as a ST seat in the ensuing general Election for the Imphal Page |4 Municipal
Corporation, 2022 in the larger interest of the general public residing in the said Ward No. 19 as an
open/unreserved seat to meet the ends of justice.
[5] He further represented that he has given proper representation on 19.10.2022 and their
grievances but without considering their grievances, the respondents have passed the present
impugned order dated 29.03.2023.
[6] Aggrieved by the same, he has filed the present writ petition before this Court to challenge the
said impugned order dated 29.03.2023. Therefore, he prayed this Court to admit this writ petition
and grant interim order.
[7] Mr. M. Rarry, learned Special Counsel appearing for the State and Mr. U. Augusta, learned
junior counsel to Mr. Y. Nirmolchand, learned senior counsel for the respondent No.3, the Imphal
Municipal Corporation take notice and appeared. [8] Mr. Rarry, learned Special Counsel for the
State has stated that the petitioners have not given any proper representation to the respondents
since the Respondents have called the objection from the public but they have challenged the
present impugned order dated 29.03.2023 without any valid ground. Therefore, he prayed this
Court to dismiss the writ petition.

Page |5 [9] Mr. U. Augusta, learned junior counsel to Mr. Y. Nirmolchand, learned senior counsel
for the respondent No.3, the Imphal Municipal Corporation has represented that he has adopted the
argument advanced by Mr. M. Rarry.
[10] Mr. N. Jotendro, the learned senior counsel represented that the petitioners have given the
representation dated 19.10.2022 and the respondents have not considered the same and he prayed
this Court that the petitioners may be directed to give a fresh representation to vindicate their
grievances and the respondents are ready to pass fresh order.
[11] Mr. Rarry represented that he is strongly opposing to grant any relief as sought for by the
petitioners and prayed to dismiss the writ petition.
[12] Considering the nature of arguments advanced by both the parties, I am inclined to pass the
following orders :
i) this writ petition is allowed.
ii) the impugned order dated 29.03.2023 is set aside.
iii) the petitioners are directed to give fresh
representations to the respondents on or before 18.04.2023 Page |6
iv) and on receipt of the same, the respondents No. 1 and 2 are hereby directed to pass appropriate
speaking orders on or before 24.04.2023. [13] With the above observation and directions, this writ
petition is disposed of.
[14] Registry is directed to issue copy of this order to both the parties today itself.
