This Court admitted the appeal on 20.10.2023, however, substantial questions of law were not
framed. Vide order dated 17.10.2023 in MC (RSA) No.21 of 2023, this Court permitted insertion of
para 7A in the Memo of Appeal, raising five substantial questions of law.
After hearing the parties and on perusal of the case record, the following substantial question of law
have been framed.
i) Whether the unregistered sale deed dated 14.06.1993 signed only by the Seller and
not signed by the purchaser can be used for collateral purposes to claim adverse
possession in the absence of any evidence of physical possession by the
respondents/defendants?
ii) Whether the respondents/defendants can claim their ownership over the suit land on the basis of
the title and by way of adverse possession?
iii) Whether limitation under Article 65 of the Limitation Act starts from 14.06.1993 i.e. from the
date of the alleged execution of the unregistered sale deed dated 14.6.1993 or from 14.03.2011 i.e.
the day the Appellantplaintiff came to know about the entries of the names of the Defendant No.3
vide Mutation Case No.17/AS&SO (IE-II) dated 27.03.2010?

iv) Whether in the absence of any evidence of physical possession of the suit land by the

v) Whether the Appellant/plaintiff has cause of action for filing the suit?

substantial questions of law framed in terms of Sub Section (5) of Section 100 of the CPC at the time
