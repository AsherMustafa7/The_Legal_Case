BEFORE HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE A. GUNESHWAR SHARMA 01.03.2023 Heard Mr. Th.
Sanachaoba, learned PP for Customs, Government of India.
[2] The present Criminal Revision Petition is filed against the order dated 22.03.2021 passed by the
Ld. Chief Judicial Magistrate, Chandel in Cil.(C) Case No.09 of 2019 where the respondent/accused
was convicted under Section 135(1)(b) & Section 135(1)(b)(ii) of the Custom Act, 1962. The
respondent/accused was sentenced to imprisonment till the rising of the Court and to pay a fine of
Rs.10,00,000 (Rupees ten thousand) only and on default, another imprisonment of 15 days.
Being aggrieved by the impugned order, the Cril.Rev.Petn. has been filed on inadequacy of the
quantum of punishment. Vide order dated 24.02.2023 in MC(Cril.Rev.P.) No.15 of 2022, this Court
condoned the delay of 15 days in filing the present Cril.Rev.Petn. [3] On perusal of the memo of
revision, it is seen that this is for enhancement of the sentence passed by the Ld. Chief Judicial
Magistrate, Chandel and is filed as Cril.Revision Petition under Section 397 of Cr.P.C. However,
Section 377 of Cr.P.C. provided for appeal against sentence and the present Cril.Revision Petition
ought to be filed as an appeal against sentence.

In the interest of justice, the present Cril.Rev.Petition is treated and converted to Criminal Appeal
and the same may be registered as Appeal under Section 377 of Cr.P.C. Registry is directed to do the
needful. [4] The petitioner is to make necessary correction in the memo of Appeal.
[5] The petitioner is directed to take steps upon the respondent.
[6] List on 5th April, 2023.
