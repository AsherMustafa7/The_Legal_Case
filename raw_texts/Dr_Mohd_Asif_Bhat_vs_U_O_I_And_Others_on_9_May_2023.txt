ORDER
1. The present petition has been listed after issuance of notice way back on 09.04.2012 i.e. after
about 11 years.
2. Case set up by the petitioner is that he has applied for fresh Passport on 05.03.2010 vide File No.
A002603 and requisite fee was deposited under Passport Rules. He was informed by the Passport
Office to furnish desired information/documents within one month, failing which his application
shall be treated closed. Pursuant thereto, he submitted character certificate issued by S.S.P. Jammu.
3. It is submitted that petitioner is a Veterinary Assistant Surgeon and intends to visit abroad in
search of knowledge and on pilgrimage. On 29.02.2012, on being asked about progress made in his
case with respect to issuance of passport, he was simply informed that same was not recommended
by respondent Nos. 2 and 3 without assigning any reason. According to the petitioner, he has been
deprived of his Constitutional Right to have international Passport.
4. It is submitted that on the basis of his personal character and clear service record, he was earlier
permitted to go on pilgrimage of Haj in the year 2008 vide Cover No : JK-5973-3, Passport No.
94909.
5. The grievance of the petitioner is that despite having no adverse report against him in any Police
record and despite having no affiliation with any Political Party or involvement in political or
subversive activities throughout his career, he has been denied to issue passport without any
justification.

6. Mr. Vishal Sharma, learned DSGI appearing on behalf of respondents submits that, in case,
petitioner applies for fresh Passport, respondents may be directed to consider the case of the
petitioner in accordance with law and the rules framed there under.
7. At this stage, Mr. Satinder Gupta, learned counsel for the petitioner submits that latest report
issued by S.S.P. Jammu reveals that as per the reports received from Subordinate units of District
Jammu as also from field formations, no case is registered against the petitioner in any Police
Station.
8. Having regard to what has been observed hereinabove, the present petition is disposed of with
liberty to the petitioner to apply afresh for issuance of Passport within two weeks and thereafter
respondents shall consider his case in accordance with law within a period of four weeks from the
date copy of this order is made available to them.
Disposed of.
