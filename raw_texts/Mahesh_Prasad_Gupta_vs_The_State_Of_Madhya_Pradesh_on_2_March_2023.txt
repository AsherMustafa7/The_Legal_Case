Mahesh Prasad Gupta vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 2 March, 2023
1. Petitioner has filed this writ petition under Article 226 of the Constitution of India challenging
orders dated 10.6.2019, 6.12.2019 and 27.1.2021.
2. Counsel appering for the petitioner presses his I.A.No. 12070/2022, an application for issuing
appropriate direction. He submitted that Revisional Court had issued directiong to Authorized
Officer to consider the application of petitioner for compounding of offence. It is submitted that
petitioner has filed an application before the competent authority but same has not been considered
and decided and his application is pending.
3. Govt. Advocate for the State submitted that value of timber is more than Rs. 1000/-, therefore,
same cannot be compounded.
4. Heard learned counsel for the parties.
5. Application filed by the petitioner is pending before the competent authority and same has not
been decided. Respondents/Authorities are under duty to comply with the order which has been
passed by Revisional Court and take a decision on the application filed by the petitioner.
6. In view of same, petitioner is granted liberty to file fresh application for compounding of offence
before the competent authority. On filing the said application, Competent Authority shall consider
the application for compounding in accordance with law. Reasoned order shall be passed on the
application and same be communicated to petitioner.
7. With the aforesaid direction, writ petition is disposed off. Order passed by the Revisional
Authority is not interfered with.
(VISHAL DHAGAT) JUDGE DUBEY/-
