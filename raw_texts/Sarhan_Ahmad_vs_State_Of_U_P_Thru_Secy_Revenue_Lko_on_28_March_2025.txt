dispensed with.
The instant petition has been preferred seeking following main relief(s):-
"Wherefore, it is most respectfully prayed that this Hon'ble Court may graciously be
pleased to direct the Opposite Party No. 2, Sub Divisional Magistrate, Tehsil-Bikapur,

District-Ayodhya since 01.07.2022 which was registered as Case No. 3077/2022
(Sarhan Ahmad versus Nayab Ahmad and others) decide the pending before him a
suit for partition under Section 116 of U.P. Revenue Code more than 30 months
lapsed which is decided in expeditiously within stipulated time granted by this
Hon'ble Court in the interest of justice."
Taking note of Rule 109(10) of U.P. Revenue Code Rules, 2016 made under the U.P. Revenue Code,
2006, as per which, a suit for partition preferred under Section 116 of the Code of 2006 should
preferably be decided within a period of six months, however, in the instant case, the case, in issue,
has not been disposed of till date despite the fact that the same is pending since July, 2022 and on
account of pendency of the same, the petitioner, as stated, who is aged about 50 years, is suffering
great hardship and mental agony, this Court is of the view that no fruitful purpose will be served in
keeping this petition pending.
Accordingly, this petition is disposed of with a direction to the respondent No.2/Sub Divisional
Magistrate, Tehsil- Bikapur, District- Ayodhya to consider and decide theCase No. 3077/2022
(Sarhan Ahmad versus Nayab Ahmad and others) most expeditiously after affording full
opportunity of hearing to the parties to the litigation and without granting unnecessary
adjournment to either party preferably within a period of six months from the next date fixed in the
case, if there is no other legal impediment in this regard.
It is made clear that the Court has not examined the case of either party on merits and the Authority
concerned shall be free to decide the matter strictly in accordance with law.
With the aforesaid, the petition is disposed of.
