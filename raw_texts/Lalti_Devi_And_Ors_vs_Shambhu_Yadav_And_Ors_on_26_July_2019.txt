3. Briefly stated the facts of the case as disclosed in the claim petition is that claimants are widow
and children of Munna Choudhary, who was helper in the Truck No. BR-1GA-9183 who died in
motor accident on 03.06.2010 at 12:30 a.m. when said truck met an accident. Deceased was 27 years
old and was having income of Rs. 4,500/- per month and died due to rash and negligent driving of
driver of said truck. The driver. The driver was holding a valid driving license. The offending truck
was owned by opposite party no.1 and insured by opposite party no.3 Oriental Insurance Co. Ltd.
4. F.I.R. was instituted against the driver of offending vehicle giving rise to Simri P.S. Case No. 171 of
2010 and after completion of investigation police submitted chargesheet against the driver of the
offending vehicle.
5. Opposite party no.3 insurance company appeared and filed their written statement denying the
claim and claim case to be not maintainable. Offending vehicle collided with standing Dumper and
there was contributory negligence and owner of the both vehicle are liable to pay Patna High Court
MA No.72 of 2015 dt.26-07-2019 the compensation. Opposite party no. 1 and 2 who are the owner
and driver of the vehicle did not appear in spite of valid service of notice and the case proceeded ex
parte against them.
6. On the basis of pleading of the parties, the Tribunal framed six issue. In support of her claim,
three witnesses were examined by the claimant. AW-1 Lalti Devi is claimant, AW-2 Somaru
Choudhary is claimant and AW-3 is Manish Kumar. Claimants have also produced documentary
evidence in support of their claim case. Exhibit-1 is certified copy of F.I.R. of Simri P.S. Case No. 171
of 2010, Exhibit-2 is certified copy of chargesheet, Exhibit-3 is Photocopy of Postmortem report,
Exhibit-4 is photocopy of insurance policy.
7. The Tribunal has held that due to rash and negligent driving of the offending vehicle by the driver
of vehicle which dashed from behind from a standing dumper as a result of which Munna
Choudhary suffered serious injuries and subsequently succumbed to his injuries in course of
treatment in P.M.C.H. The name of the driver of offending truck is Raju Yadav and number of the
offending vehicle was BR-1GA-9183 and the number of dumper was BR-1G- Patna High Court MA
No.72 of 2015 dt.26-07-2019 9867.
8. Deceased was helper in the offending vehicle as such he was not a third party and since no
objection has been taken by the insurance company, it will be presumed that the insurance policy
covered the risk of unlimited liability of helper in the offending vehicle. Insurance company has not
filed any appeal or cross appeal.
9. On the basis of admitted facts the compensation for which claimants are entitled is reassessed by
this Court.
