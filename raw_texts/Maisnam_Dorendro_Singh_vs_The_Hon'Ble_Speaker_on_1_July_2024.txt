Mr. Ajoy Pebam, learned counsel, appears on behalf of the petitioner; and Mr. Lenin
Hijam, learned Advocate General, Manipur, appears on behalf of the Manipur
Legislative Assembly.
The present writ petition under Article 226 of the Constitution of India, has been
instituted by Maisnam Dorendro Singh, statedly, the Secretary General, Janata Dal
(United), praying as follows:-
"In the premises aforesaid it is most respectfully prayed that your Lordships may be
pleased to: -
i) issue notice:
ii) allow the Petitioner to take steps to the Private Respondent Nos. 2 and 7 through
their email ngursanglur@gmail.com and lmkhaute 2021@gmail.com respectively as
well as through SP, Churachandpur/DGP, Manipur,
iii) admit this Petition;
iv) issue Rule nisi;

vii) award the cost of the petition.
And, for this act of kindness the humble Petitioner as in duty bound shall ever remain pray."
Mr. Lenin Hijam, learned Advocate General, Manipur, by inviting our attention to paragraph 213.2
of the decision of the Constitution Bench of the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India in Subhash Desai
Vs. Principal Secretary, Governor of Maharashtra and Others, reported as (2024) 2 SCC 719, states
on behalf of the Hon'ble Speaker, Manipur Legislative Assembly, that the latter shall adjudicate the
petition instituted on behalf of the petitioner for disqualification of the subject MLAs under the
Tenth Schedule within a reasonable period of time, in accordance with law.
The statement made on behalf of the Hon'ble Speaker is hereby accepted. Directed accordingly.
No further relief is prayed for.
With the above direction, the writ petition is disposed of. Needless to state that in the event of any
difficulty, the petitioner is at liberty to revive the writ petition.
