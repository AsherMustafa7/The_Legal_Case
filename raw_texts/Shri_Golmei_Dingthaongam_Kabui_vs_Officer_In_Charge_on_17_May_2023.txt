BEFORE HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE A. GUNESHWAR SHARMA 17.05.2023 [1] Heard Mr. M.
Gunedhor, learned counsel for the applicant; and Mr. H. Samarjit, learned PP for the State.
[2] This matter is taken up today on mention as none appeared on 04.05.2023 due to the curfew
imposed by the State Government. [3] Mr. M. Gunedhor, learned counsel for the applicant, states
that the applicant is the complainant in F.I.R. No. 8(4) 2023 SPT-PS u/s 21(C)/60(3) NDPS Act
1985 & 384 IPC regarding involvement of 3 persons and 2 VDF personnel in connection with the
seizure of 79(seventy-nine) soap cases containing suspected brown sugar and the released of the 3
accused persons by 2 VDF personnel on alleged payment of Rs. 1(one) lakh through GPay. In the
complaint lodged by the applicant herein, the payment of Rs. 1(one) lakh by the accused person to
one of the VDF Personnel is also mentioned. Later on, the applicant is also implicated as an accused
in the present case on the disclosure by 2 VDF personnel that Rs. 1(one) lakh was taken on his
instigation. The applicant approached the Court of learned Special Judge (NDPS), Senapati, by way
of an application being Cril. Misc. (AB) Case No. 13 of 2023 under Section 438 Cr.P.C and vide order
dated 17.04.2023, he was given interim protection. Vide order dated 26.04.2023, the application
under Section 438 Cr.P.C was rejected mainly on the ground that the 3 accused persons and the
contraband along with vehicle were released after payment of Rs. 1(one) lakh on the instigation by
the present applicant. Being aggrieved by the order dated 26.04.2023, the applicant approached this
Court by way of the present application on the ground that the allegation made against him is totally

[4] Issue notice.
[5] Mr. H. Samarjit, learned PP, accepts notice on behalf of
respondent No. 1 and no formal notice is required. [6] Respondent No. 2, who is the original
complainant of the F.I.R. is the applicant himself who is the original complainant in the F.I.R. and is
impleaded in compliance of the High Court Rules as the complainant had to be impleaded. In the
present case, the complainant himself is the petitioner and hence respondent No. 2 be deleted from
the array of parties. [7] Mr. H. Samarjit, learned PP, seeks one weeks' time to file objections in the
bail application.
[8] Mr. M. Gunedhor, learned counsel for the applicant, prays that interim protection may be given
till the next date as he is the original complainant and he was also on interim bail before the Trial
Court. [9] Considering this, interim protection is given to the applicant till the next date. In case, the
applicant is arrested, he shall be released on bail bond of Rs. 1,00,000/- (Rupees One Lakh) with
one surety (Government employee) to the satisfaction of the arresting authority subject to
conditions that:-
(a) The petitioner shall furnish personal bond of Rs.
1,00,000/- (Rupees One Lakh) with one surety of the like amount.
(b) The petitioner shall co-operate with the investigation.
(c) The petitioner shall not tamper with the evidence or influence witnesses.
(d) The petitioner shall not leave Manipur without permission of Ld. Special Judge (NDPS),
Senapati.
(e) On breach of any of the aforesaid conditions, the respondent police is entitled to take appropriate
action against the petitioner in accordance with law. [10] Fix on 25.05.2023 for further proceedings.
[11] Furnish a copy of this order to the learned counsel appearing for the parties.
