BEFORE HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE A.GUNESHWAR SHARMA 18.10.2024 Heard Mr.T.Rajendra,
learned senior counsel assisted by Mr.Bikash Sharma, learned counsel for the appellants and
Mr.Sachindra, learned counsel for respondent Nos.1-5.
Service to respondent Nos.6 and 7 who are proforma defendant Nos.6 and 7 before the Trial Court
has been sent by Speed Post.
Appellants are directed to submit tracking report on the next date.
After perusing the materials on record for the parties, Appeal is admitted on the following
substantial questions of law:
(1) Whether the suit is not maintainable in view of Section 159 of the Manipur Land
Revenue & Land Reforms Act, 1960?
(2) Whether the decree for eviction and demolition of structures of defendant Nos.1-4
is barred by Article 65 of the Limitation Act?
(3) Whether the decree for eviction and demolition of structures of defendant Nos.1-4 is in
compliance of Order IV Rule I, Sub Sections (2) and (3) CPC?
(4) Whether the suit land is the inherited properties of the parties to the extent of half-half shares
through deceased Gurumayum Ibungobi Sharma and Gurumayum Babu Sharma or not?

(5) Whether the defendant No.1 or 1-4 are the joint owners of the half share portion of the deceased
G.babu Sharma as pleaded in the plaint of the plaintiff or not?
(6) Whether the plaintiffs have locus-standi to file the suit or not?
(7) Whether the plaintiff have to establish his own case irrespective of the deficiency of the

(8) Whether both the Courts below have passed the decree in absence of necessary party?
Mr.Sachindra, learned counsel for the respondents submits that he has reserved the right to argue
as to whether the questions so framed are not the substantial questions of law in terms of Sub
Section (5) of Section 100 of the CPC.
