5|Page [3] Mr. S. Sachindra, learned counsel for the petitioners represented that the petitioners
have filed the writ petitions with a simple prayer for directing the respondent No.1, the
Commissioner (Art & Culture), Government of Manipur to take final decisions on the two
applications submitted by the petitioners on 28.01.2022 (Annexure-A/8 to the writ petition in
WP(C) No. 269 of 2022) and 31.01.2022 (Annexure-A/8 to the writ petition in WP(C) No. 268 of
2022) and the same is pending without any further progress. Therefore, he prayed this Court to pass
appropriate orders by directing the respondent No. 1 to pass appropriate speaking orders on the
applications submitted by the petitioners on 28.01.2022 and 31.01.2022 as stated hereinabove.
[4] On the other hand, Mrs. L. Monomala, learned GA for the respondents No. 1, 2 and 3
represented that the applications have been filed by the petitioners and the enquiry is yet to
complete and the enquiry report will be submitted soon. Mr. Th. Jugindro, learned counsel for the
respondents No. 4 and 5 seeks time to file counter affidavit.
[5] Considering the nature of the prayers sought for in the writ petitions, this Court is of the
considered view that no prejudice will be caused to the respondents if the respondents are directed
to pass appropriate orders on the petitioners' applications stated hereinabove.
6|Page [6] Therefore, this Court is inclined to pass the following orders :
1) the writ petitions are disposed of.
