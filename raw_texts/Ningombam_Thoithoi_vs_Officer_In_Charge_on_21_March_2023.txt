ORDER
21.03.2023 Present Mr. Irom Denning, learned counsel for the petitioner and Mr. Y. Ashang,
learned PP for State Respondent No.1. [2] It is stated that the petitioner is accused No.2 in FIR
No.11(01)2023 Porompat P/S under Section 409/420/467/468/471 120(B) IPC along with his wife
and another person.
It is stated that the petitioner is a cancer patient and has been taking treatment at the Tata
Momorial Centre, Navi, Mumbai since 09.09.2014 and he has got appointment for further
treatment on 24.03.2023 for long term follow-up treatment. The petitioner was granted interim
protection vide order dated 23.01.2023 passed by the Ld. Addl. Sessions Judge (FTC), Manipur in
Cril.Misc.(AB) Case No.2 of 2023. However, vide order dated 16.03.2023, the Ld. Addl. Sessions
Judge (FTC), Manipur has rejected the bail application on the ground that the case involved 36
transactions of gold ornament by forgery. It is further observed that the petitioner is not able to
produce any document to show that he is receiving/undergoing treatment for the past few years as a
cancer patient. [3] Mr. Irom Denning, learned counsel for the petitioner submitted that 44
documents were annexed before the Ld. Addl. Sessions Judge (FTC), Manipur and the same was not
considered.

[4] Issue notice.
[5] Mr. Y. Ashang, learned PP accepts notice on behalf of the State
Respondent No.1. No formal notice is required in respect of Respondent No.1.
[6] The Petitioner is directed to take steps to Respondent Nos.2 to 5. [7] Mr. Irom Denning, learned
counsel for the petitioner submits that interim protection may be given to the petitioner with the
permission to leave station to take treatment at the Tata Momorial Centre, Navi, Mumbai as
scheduled on 24.03.2023.
[8] Considering the submissions, it is directed that,
a) the petitioner namely, Ningombam Thoithoi (42), S/o. N. Bijoy Singh of Wangkhei Angom Leikai,
PO & PS Porompat, Imphal East District, be released on interim bail in case of arrest in connection
with FIR No.11(01)2023 Porompat P/S under Section 409/420/467/468/471 120(B) IPC.
b) the petitioner is directed to pay a surety bond of Rs.50,000/-
(Rupees fifty thousand) only with like amount to the satisfaction of the I.O. in FIR No.11(01) 2023
Porompat P/S.
c) the petitioner is directed not to tamper with the evidences or influence the witnesses.
d) the petitioner is permitted to leave station in regard to treatment and on return, he shall report to
the I/O of the case.
e) if any of the conditions is violated by the petitioner, the respondent police are at liberty to
approach this Court for cancellation of bail. [9] Mr. Y. Ashang, learned PP seeks 2(two) weeks' time
to file objection.
[10] Fix on 4th April, 2023.
[11] Copy of this order may be furnished to the learned counsels for
the parties.
[12] Send a copy of the order to the Officer-in-Charge, Porompat
Police Station for information.
