"1. List revised.

3. This is the third Anticipatory Bail Application. The first anticipatory bail

"Sarwar and 5 others Vs. State of U.P." in which Gulam Varis, the present applicant

2023 "Gulam Varis Vs. State of U.P." which was rejected by this Court vide order
dated 5.4.2023 since the same did not contain the necessary material as it was filed
after submission of charge sheet.
4. Learned counsel for the applicant argued that the applicant was not arrested
during investigation. The trial court has rejected the anticipatory bail application of
the applicant vide order dated 6.12.2022 by observing that after filing of charge sheet
only summons have been issued against the applicant but no warrant has been issued
against him and as such there is no apprehension of his arrest. It is submitted that
since the applicant had cooperated in the investigation during it's pendency and was
not arrested and now charge sheet has been submitted and he apprehends his arrest
pursuant to summons issued by the trial court the applicant may be granted interim
anticipatory bail.

7. Issue notice to the first informant in accordance with law returnable within six

8. Steps be taken within a week.

(i) the applicant shall make himself available on each and every date fixed in the

promise to any person acquainted with the facts of the case so as to dissuade him
from disclosing such facts to the Court.
(iii) the applicant shall not leave India without the previous permission of the Court
and if he/she has passport, the same shall be deposited before the concerned court.
12. In case, the applicant does not co-operate in the proceedings of the trial, this
order shall stand automatically recalled/vacated and the applicant shall be taken into
custody, forthwith.
13. Further, in default of any of the conditions, the court concerned is at liberty to
pass appropriate orders for enforcing and compelling the same."

Kushinagar, during the period of trial for the reasons as given in the said order on his furnishing a
