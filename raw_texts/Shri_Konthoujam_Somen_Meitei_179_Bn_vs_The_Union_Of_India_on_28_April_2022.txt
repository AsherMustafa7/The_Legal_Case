Page - 2 [2] The writ petitioner has filed the present writ petitioner seeking the prayer as follows:-
(i) Admit the writ petition and issue nisi calling upon the respondents to show cause
as to why a writ/order/direction in the nature of Certiorari or any other appropriate
Writ/Order/directive (S) should not be for quashing and setting aside the office order
bearing No.P.VIII-4/2017-Est-II/179 dated 15/12/2017 (at Annexure-A/3) whereby
the petitioner was dismissed from service and to reinstate the petitioner back into
service with back wages on humane ground, in the facts and circumstance of the
present petition;
(ii) To show cause as to why appropriate writ/order/directives in the nature of
Mandamus/Certiorari should not be issued directing the respondents to consider and
dispose of the Appeal dated 30th July, 2020(Annexure-A/4)/
(iii) Make the Rule Absolute;
(iv) to pass any appropriate order or direction(s) which may be appropriate to be
passed under such facts and circumstance of the instant case, for the ends of justice.
(v) Your Lordship's may be gracious enough to pass appropriate interim order
staying/suspending the illegal and arbitrary dismissal order dated 15/12/2017 (at
Annexure-A/3), for the ends of justice may call for.
[3] Before filing the present writ petition in WP(C)No.717 of 2020, the writ petitioner has filed an
appeal before the authority Page - 3 concerned. During pendency of the said appeal, the writ
petitioner approached this Court by filing the present writ petition in WP(C)No.717 of 2020
challenging the order dated 15.12.2017. While pendency of the present writ petition in WP(C) No.717
of 2020, it is reported that the concerned authority passed an order rejecting the appeal on
25.03.2021 filed by the petitioner.
[4] Mr. M. Devananda, learned counsel appearing for the petitioner represented that though the
appeal was dismissed on 25.03.2021 but the copy of the same was served on him only on 27.04.2022
and he represented that the delay was occurred on the part of the respondents by not serving the
copy of the orders and therefore, he is unable to challenge the same.
[5] Accordingly, the learned counsel appearing for the petitioner Mr. M. Devananda represented he
may be permitted to withdraw the present writ petition in WP(C) No. 717 of 2020 by giving liberty to
challenge the order and the appeal order. [6] Accordingly, this writ petition is dismissed with liberty
to file fresh writ petition challenging the orders and the appeal order.
