1. Heard Shri Raj Kr. Singh Suryvanshi, learned counsel for the petitioner, learned Additional Chief
Standing Counsel for the State and Shri Shikhar Anand, learned counsel for the State Information
Commission.
2. The petitioner wants benefit of the order dated 11.10.2018 passed in Writ Petition No. 29863
(M/B) of 2018 which was decided in the following terms:-
"Heard Sri Rishabh Raj, learned Counsel for the petitioner, learned Standing Counsel
and Sri Shikhar Anand, learned Counsel for the State Information Commission.
The contention of the petitioner is that the impugned order imposing a penalty of
Rs.25,000/- and also giving certain directions has been passed without giving an

opportunity of hearing to the petitioner or any notice to it.
On being confronted, learned Counsel for the Commission Sri Shikhar Anand pointed
out the provisions of Rule 12 of the U.P. Right to Information Rules, 2015 and
contended that if it so, then an application for recall of the order is maintainable
before the Commission at the behest of the petitioner.
In view of the aforesaid submission and the Rule position, this writ petition is
disposed of with liberty to the petitioner to approach the Commission under Rule 12
of the Rules 2015 by moving appropriate application. If such application is filed
within two weeks, the same shall be considered and decided expeditiously for a
period of two months.
The penalty imposed by the impugned order shall not be recovered from the
petitioner till two months."
3. This writ petition is disposed of in terms of above quoted order and the benefit granted in the
aforesaid case shall be available to the petitioner herein on the same terms.
